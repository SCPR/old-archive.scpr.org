100-degree heat wave in store for SoCal

A heat wave is expected to strike Southern California this week with temperatures topping 100 degrees in L.A. by Wednesday.

Strong Santa Ana winds more common in October and November but not unheard of in May are expected to accompany the heat, prompting red flag warnings for increased fire danger. Its expected to be hottest from Tuesday to Friday, with temperatures 15-20 degrees higher than average.

OC can look to other counties on Laura's Law

The Orange County Board of Supervisors is set to vote Tuesday on whether to adopt Lauras Law, which would allow the county to force some people with severe mental illness into treatment.

Nevada County has already implemented the law. John Buck, who heads a service provider there, says he knows the involuntary nature of the law is controversial, but it works. "All were asking them to do is actually engage with us," he explains, adding they have a menu of services to choose from, including psychotherapy, nursing, housing support, benefits and medication.

LAPD officer killed on street where officer died in March

An off-duty LAPD officer was killed in Beverly Hills Friday afternoon when his car was hit by a cement truck. It happened on the 1000 block of Loma Vista, the same street where LAPD officer Nicholas Lee was killed by an errant dump truck back in March.

This is the fourth traffic-related death of an LAPD officer in recent months. Mayor Eric Garcetti offered his condolences in a statement. Fighting back tears at a press conference, Chief Charlie Beck said he'd known the officer, "a fine family man," for decades.

$2.5M First 5 grant coaxes poor families to buy produce

Child advocacy group First 5 LA has provided a $2.5 million grant to expand Market Match, a program that encourages low-income families that use food stamps to buy fruits and vegetables. Families who use them to buy produce at certain farmers markets are given an extra dollar for each dollar they spend, in some cases up to $10 per week.

The program is already in 14 farmers markets in L.A. County. It's being added to the Compton, Vermont Village, Valinda and La Cienega markets, with a goal of having the program in 37 of them within five years.

Wilmore of 'Daily Show' to replace Colbert

The late night TV shuffle continues, with Larry Wilmore set to take over the time slot currently held by Stephen Colbert, future host of "Late Show." Wilmore, a veteran comedy writer and current "senior black correspondent" on "The Daily Show" will helm and executive produce "The Minority Report" with Jon Stewart's Bus Boy Productions.

Wilmore's new show, which begins next January, will be one of the few late night shows hosted by an African American, and his show will "offer a comedic look at news, current events and pop culture from unique perspectives not typically on display in late night television."

Study: Ireland, Europe getting fatter

A study from the World Health Organization is predicting that Ireland will be the fattest country in Europe by 2030, with a staggering 90% of men and 84% of women projected to be classified as overweight or obese.

The rest of Europe won't fare too well either: based on body mass index measurements and computer modeling, obesity rates are expected to rise in nearly all 53 Eurozone countries. Not surprisingly, the lack of healthy eating habits and exercise are the main culprits. The economic downturn has also forced people to consume cheaper, less-healthy food.

Marianne Williamson endorsed by former rival

TV producer Brent Roske has dropped out of the race for the Congressional seat soon to be vacated by Henry Waxman, and has thrown his support to one-time rival New Age spiritualist-cum-candidate Marianne Williamson.

Roske's surprise announcement was filmed at Williamson's headquarters and uploaded to YouTube. He asked Republicans, Democrats and Libertarians to make a "bold, historic vote on June 3 and vote for my friend, Marianne Williamson." Sixteen other candidates are running for Waxman's seat.

First openly gay player drafted in pro football

Michael Sam, who came out as gay in media interviews earlier this year, was picked by the St. Louis Rams in the 7th round of the NFL draft Saturday. His team and coaches had kept his secret for his final college season. Sam was Southeastern Conference defensive player of the year.

The 6-foot-2, 255-pound Sam was considered a mid-to-late round pick, far from a sure thing to be drafted. At a time when gay marriage is gaining acceptance among Americans, Sam's entry into the NFL is seen as a huge step toward the integration of gay men into professional team sports.

Florida man wants Satanist prayer at council meeting

A Florida rabble-rouser wants the city of Deerfield Beach to allow him to say a satanist prayer at the beginning of a council meeting. Chaz Stevens' request comes after the Supreme Court ruled that sectarian prayer before a government meeting doesn't necessarily violate the Constitution's Establishment Clause.

In 2013, Stevens was allowed to set up a Festivus pole made of empty beer cans in the Capitol. What Stevens wrote that he might read: "O mighty Lord Satan, / teach us to become strong and wise! / Teach us to vanquish the enemies / of our freedom and well-being!"

Drag queen Conchita Wurst wins Eurovision song contest

Austrian bearded drag queen Conchita Wurst won the 59th edition of the Eurovision Song Contest Saturday with James Bond-inspired power ballad "Rise Like a Phoenix."

"This is dedicated to everyone who believes in a future of peace and freedom," a tearful Wurst said as she accepted the trophy. Wurst is the alter ego of 25-year-old Thomas Neuwirth.

The first Eurovision song contest was held in 1956 in Switzerland and over the years, the contest's most famous winners include ABBA and Celine Dion. Watch the video.
